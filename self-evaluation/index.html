Self Evaluation

If you have ever tried to use a GPS, you know that you cannot get to where you want to go unless you know where you are now. Take the time to explore this self-evaluatiom. It will provide you with an insight into what you know, what you are good at, and maybe help you identify some opportunities for improvement so that you can become a highly effective pitch presenter.

Click to watch two pitch presentations. Watch and contrast them. What do you notice? What is different? What makes the second one better than the first?

Click to rate where you think your skills are in relation to delivering a pitch presentation. Be honest with yourself, this is for your eyes only. If your not sure - ask others for feedback and insight before completing the quiz.

Self Quiz

Name:
Never Rarely Sometimes Often Always
1. I have a clear understanding of what is important about my
topic to my audience.
2. I am highly effective at translating my content into clear
value propositions for all stakeholder, including
my audience.
3. I take the time to plan and prepare my presentation/pitch.
4. I demonstrate that I have a clear understanding of the
problems, risks, commercial return or needs my audience
considers important.
5. I clearly articulate the commercial and business strategy,
plan and value that my solution/proposal provides to
my audience.
6. I demonstrate that I have a robust understanding of the
market, needs/problems, risks, and how to reach the
addressable market.
7. I clearly show a return and provide a clear exit strategy and timeline for my audience.
8. I demonstrate why we are the right people to help solve
the problem for the user and for the investore/partner.
9. My presentations have a clear introduction that ensures
the audience understands who we are, what we are
talking about, why this of iportant to them, with a
clear agenda and outcome or call to action.
10. I adjust the style of my presentation based on the communications style of my audience, taking into account:
• Level of detail
• Amount of jargon and technical detail
• Level of energy in my presentation
11. I appreciate my presentation/pitch and seek objective
feedback so that I can keep improving.
12. I make focused eye contact with my audience.
13. I use pausing for effect.
14. I move with purpose.
15. I command the space.
16. I talk with authority.
17. I vary my voice to create interest and emotion.
